My Thoughts about LGBTQ in Sydney

From the news about that one student from Ji Nan University in Canton appealed to the court that gay should be classified as mental disorder, we can see that if the authorities of mental health have the prejudice of the group of sexual minorities, it is very common to have the prejudice among the citizens. Once I searched about the question about which group of people need to immigrate to foreign countries in China in Zhihu (China’s Reddit),the most popular answer is “ the corruptive authorities and the LGBTQ group”. Once I discussed this problem with some open-minded friends, most of whom hold the attitudes that they will tolerate them but they won't support the group. I discussed with a girl who is bisexual and she told me that she wouldn’t express the likeness for a girl since she has the concern that the girl will consider her as a jerk.Since there aren’t any particular non-profit underground organisations at universities are in support of the sexual minorities, she considers she can only find boys as her companions under the objection of her parents. One touching thing that changed my mind is that there is a gay called C in my french classes.He is a senior partner in a law firm and he is a law professor at a university. When we had our second french class, he told us he is a gay and he is the co-founder of two sexual-minority organisations. He is friendly, smart, rich, owning a high social status, belonging to the high class in Sydney, different from what most of Chinese would have considered gay group are. To be honest, before getting a full understanding of sexual minorities, my impression of this group came from Chai Qing’s book “insight“ (a biography written by a Chinese journalist who has worked in China’s press authorities for more than 20 years), which described them as patients of AIDS, drug dealers, marginalized people. I am in support of most of Chai’s point of views, but not this one. I believe among China’s elites, some of them may be sexual minorities. However, as a result of the common sense of the whole society which is not friendly to the sexual minorities, the social elites won’t speak out for themselves, nevertheless the ordinary people. Now, I would like to introduce the classification of the sexual minorities. The sexual minorities are universally recognised as LGBTQ group. L stands for lesbian, G stands for gay, B stands for bisexual, T stands for transgender, Q stands for queer, I stands for intersex, and A stands for asexual.
I would like to emphasise that the sexual preferences of the sexual minorities are different from others doesn’t mean that they are freak.Here is a not so appropriate metaphor: just like any of us has our unique preferences for music.It doesn’t mean that the people who listen to classical music are more noble than the people who listen to the classical music. In conclusion, it’s the diversity of people which colours our world.If we don’t speak for the rights of the people now, we may lose the right to speak for other rights in the future.

Feminism in my heart

I am a feminist since I was very young. “Feminism is a range of social movements, political movements, and ideologies that aim to define and establish the political, economic, personal, and social equality of the sexes”, according to the definition of feminism in Wikipedia. As an “unconventional ” Chinese girl, I used to have super short hair, hang around with boys and run a lot on the playground. Since from nowhere, my aunts and uncles started to remind me to become more “girly”,such as have longer hair and play with the dolls.When some cousins of the same age came to my home, they would said,these boys might have better achievements than you, although you got higher scores than them right now. I seldom listened to their so-called opinions and I tried to follow my heart and be myself.When I finally entered one of the top high schools in Canton and chose to study science instead of art,I thought I finally got the chance to compete with the boys fairly.However, I had no idea that my physics teacher commented in class that all the girls in our science class should transfer to study art.Afterwards, he said that it was a kind of encouragement by putting pressure on the girls.In my opinion, he was just unaware of how serious this matter would be. My root of feminism started with the speech of Emma Watson at the United Nations “ HE FOR SHE”.Her words “If not me, who?If not now, when?” touched me so deeply that the effect was huge and far-reaching. In my opinion, people in western countries have higher awareness of respecting the women’s rights than in China.For example, my friends for Norway told me that in their kindergarten, either boys or girls have learn woodworking and textile.The teacher don’t say that boys like blue or girls like pink.Another example is that to promote the employment rate of women in STEM industry, many technology companies will give girls more chances when they give interviews. To get to know more about the feminism, I recently read a book called “The Second Sex“ by Simone de Beauvoir.I hope I can set a role model for the girls and women surrounding me.
